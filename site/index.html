labor.fun

Drop it into your chats. Connect your platforms. It does the rest.

A teammate you drop into your chats — it remembers, schedules, answers, and does the work. No dashboard to live in. No code. It introduces itself in your channel and sets itself up in conversation.

Add to Slack — free Connect Telegram

Free tier, no card required. Discord support coming soon.

The chat is the interface

There's no setup wizard because there's nothing to set up. You talk to it like you'd talk to a new hire.

01

Connect your chat platform

Add it to Slack or Telegram in a couple of clicks. It joins the channels you invite it to — nothing more.

02

It introduces itself

It says hello, asks what your team works on, and configures itself conversationally. Preferences, tone, access — all settled in chat.

03

Put it to work

Delegate tasks, schedule reminders, ask questions. It remembers everything per channel and picks up where you left off.

A real agent, not an autocomplete

Every conversation gets its own sandboxed agent with memory, tools, and access you control.

[mem]

Persistent memory

Remembers context, decisions, and preferences per channel and group. No re-explaining.

[agt]

Does real work

Each conversation runs a full agent in an isolated, sandboxed container — it can research, write, and execute.

[cron]

Schedules in plain English

"Every Monday at 9, post the sprint summary." Recurring tasks and reminders, no cron syntax.

[kb]

Team knowledge base

Builds and maintains a shared knowledge base from your conversations — people, tasks, artifacts.

[gh]

GitHub & Linear

Files issues, reviews PRs, updates project boards, and reports back — right in the thread.

[rec]

Meeting transcripts

Drop in a transcript; get a summary, decisions, and action items assigned to the right people.

[chn]

Multi-platform

Works across Slack and Telegram today. Discord is on the way. One teammate, everywhere you talk.

[rbac]

Role-based access

Fine-grained control over who can see what and ask for what. Privacy enforced per channel.

Built like it handles your team's data — because it does

Isolated containers

Every conversation runs in its own sandboxed container. Nothing leaks between chats, teams, or tasks.

Credentials stay outside

API keys and tokens are injected at request time and never enter the agent sandbox. The agent can use them; it can't read them.

Per-channel memory isolation

Each channel's memory is walled off. What's said in one group stays in that group.
